• Satellite analysis to enhance soil efficiency

Motivation

As we see more and more each year, the effects of climate change are becoming more notorious on our planet. Increased temperatures and changing rain patterns coupled with the effects of years of irresponsible land use are contributing to the erosion and desertification of productive land. It is estimated that global drylands could expand by as much as 23 % by the end of the 21st century and that as much as 80 % of this will occur in developing countries *(1). The loss of production this brings is hard to quantify but it is clear that it will stretch the remaining resources even thinner.

A reduction in exploitable arable land does not only signify a future scarcity of resources but also a decrease in revenue for farmers and, consequently, an expansion of the rural exodus phenomenon, posing a threat to the sustainability of rural life. *(2). It is imperative that rural communities are given all means possible to maintain their lifestyle, which provides food to people and animals. While governmental aid is a significant motivator for farming, it is not enough to keep farmers content. The approach some political entities have taken include creating job opportunities for the young, especially women; offering tax benefits; supporting entrepreneurship; modernising agricultural industry or protecting hunting.

Our solution

Our proposal is based around the idea of democratizing access to new technology in order to lower its barrier of entry. We believe that agrivoltaic systems, the cultivation of plants that can grow under solar panels, can offer a significant increase in land value and productivity, since land is not desertified to produce electricity and crops are offered a shade from the growingly adverse conditions. It has been shown that this system can increase land productivity by over 50% *(3) while saving significantly more water than traditional methods *(4).

What we offer our users, operators of medium and large agricultural landscapes and national authorities, is a service in which we do an analysis to evaluate the viability of installing photovoltaic panels and growing vegetables. Along with a recommendation on which panels and which plants are best for the area depending on factors measured with satellites. We also recommend which plants can be grown in existing solar panel parks and we do a follow-up service recommending the necessary changes in cultivation to increase soil diversity and long-term yield.

Technology

We use various products in particular from the Copernicus database like:

  • CORINE Land Cover to identify non-irrigated arable land with potential for an increased production.
  • Sentinel Hub. False color composite; to see plant density and health. NDVI (Normalized Difference Water Index) to monitor changes related to water content and as a vegetation index.
  • Local data from national and regional sources such as AEMet for land measurements such as rainfall and incident radiation.
AEMET - State Meteorological Agency

Normalized vegetation index obtained on a daily basis.

Copernicus Sentinel-2

We conduct NDVI studies over different timeframes in specific areas of interest.
